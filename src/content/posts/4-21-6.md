# April 21, 2006
I’ve been reading and rereading the music in the journal to try and piece together what I’m looking at. The music, while using the standard staff and notation, contains symbols that are completely foreign to me, like they were written as shorthand for the author to understand. Apart from the symbols, the music itself is easy enough to read but it’s nearly impossible to tell what it was actually for: no specific instruments, no titles and barely any notes, these really are mostly sketches. They’re beautiful, though, the melodies are so expressive. They have a sort of timeless quality to them, and not just because I can’t exactly date the music. I wonder who this person was and where they learned to write music like that…

I’m wondering what exactly to do with these sketches. Part of me wants to take them and turn them into full fledged songs, but another part of me feels guilty at the idea, like I’m invading someone else’s personal thoughts and feelings or something. What if these sketches were actually this person’s only lasting remains and that’s why the woman from the estate sale was so protective of the contents of the chest? That’s a lot of responsibility for me to hold, someone’s legacy, much less someone I don’t know. 

I think I won’t be comfortable doing so until I’ve read through the journal and gotten a better idea of who this person was, so my new goal is to start reading through the diary entries. My work is cut out for me though, they seem to be incredibly… antique to say the least. I’ll share more once I’ve begun the work of actually deciphering them, but the writing definitely looks like it could be a hundred years old it’s so hard to make out. Anyways, I hope to update you all again in the next week or so, talk again soon!
