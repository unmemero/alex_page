# May 07, 2006
 I have so much to unpack about my trip to Miller Creek. 

First of all, I decided against starting at the beach just cause it would be more economical for me to catch a bus to the airport and then make my way down the creek. I wanted to get an early start, but unfortunately I had to wait until I got out of work, so the sun was already high in the sky by the time I departed. Once I was there, I made my way over to the North West corner of the airport, hopped a fence, and started following the creek down. 

I have to say, if this is the river that the poem from that one journal entry is about, then the author nailed the description. The creek flows wide enough at some points that you’d have to swim across and the water glistens under the sunlight. In some patches it’s mostly tall grass and bush but in others, the scenery changes to overwhelming forest and the canopy overhead filters out most of the light. The first two hours or so were mostly uneventful, just taking as many shots as I could afford of the creek and the surrounding nature, but once I got past a few knolls of tall grass, I found it. 

![Lucet31.jpeg](/assets/images/Lucet Gallery/Lucet - 31 of 30.jpeg)
![Lucet25.jpeg](/assets/images/Lucet Gallery/Lucet - 25 of 30.jpeg)

I found a house, or rather a rotting shack that was at one point a house, but it’s the first sign of civilization I’ve found out here. It was located just under a quarter mile off the creek sitting by itself overlooking the knolls in the surrounding area. A few yards from the shack’s entrance was a small grove overrun by undergrowth and an unmarked grave, more a pile of stones than an actual tombstone. 

![Lucet32.jpeg](/assets/images/Lucet Gallery/Lucet - 32 of 30.jpeg)

It’s impossible to tell who the house or the grave belongs to and if they have any relation to the church, but I’d be lying if I said my whole body wasn’t shaking with adrenaline when I spotted it in the distance. When I was standing there in the midst of it all, I’ve never felt closer. It was beautiful there, and terribly bittersweet. I spent a while just sitting and recording with my field recorder while I took a few pictures but I ran out of film after just a few shots, which means I have to come back soon and gather more evidence. I wished I could’ve covered the entire creek, but I’ll make my way to the beach when I make my return, hopefully in the next week or so.

I’m getting closer, so close I can feel it in the back of my neck. I don’t know to what extent I will find the answers to my questions, but I at least know that I’m getting near the end of the trail. Until my next update, I’ll talk to you all soon.
