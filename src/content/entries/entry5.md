When I dream, I do not dream of this place.
I dream that we are walking hand in hand again,
Like we used to when we were children,
And we are following a river downstream to the ocean.
I dream of a thousand trees all around us and thousands of roots
Reaching towards the river, like hands reaching for Heaven.
Your hands are there too, catching me when I stumble. 
I dream of sunlight glittering through the treetops
And dancing on our faces like stained glass 
As we walk by the river towards the Ocean.
I dream that we are getting closer with each step,
I hear waves breaking and taste salty mist in the air.
It is  almost time.
 

—

I fear that I have personally disobeyed God. My mind is overactive. Perhaps this is the plight of those that stray away from God’s word. Every moment of every day I cannot help but think of myself and my songs. 

Josef and I met again. I brought up my compositions and showed him my journal. I told him that I thought it was being put to good use, but that in the process of composing and writing I may have sinned. 
We will die and become nothing anyways, he told me, so what does it matter? 
If nothing matters and we will be nothing in the end, I suppose, then it will not matter what we do from here until Lucet’s explosion. 
He gave me a smile and a nudge; he asked what we should do about it. 
I shrugged. He shrugged too. 	
I do not want to be the one that spoils your soul in my desire to live, he said. I fear that my soul can no longer be saved, though. He supposed neither of ours could - we were destined for offense. We will become nothing at the end of the year anyways, he reminded me. So, I said, I suppose we should do what we please then. He gave a shrug, a softness in his eyes appearing. 
We both want to live, so what’s stopping us, aside from ourselves, from living. Josef patted my shoulder and nodded.
